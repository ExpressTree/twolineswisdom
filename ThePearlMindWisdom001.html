THE PEARL MIND – COMIC #1

Parivartan Breaks a Rule — Moksha Responds
P
Moksha… what if one day… just one day… I break a rule?
Maybe the market looks perfect… maybe I feel confident…
What harm can come from bending a rule once?
M
The harm begins the moment you ask that question, child.
The rule is not protecting the market from you.
It is protecting you from yourself.
P
But what if the setup looks strong?
What if I feel it will work?
M
Feelings are wind.
Rules are anchors.

You do not win because you feel right.
You win because your losses never grow beyond –40.
P
So breaking the rule makes me wrong?
M
Right and wrong are not defined by what you missed…
but by what you accept.

Accept +40 or –40 only. Anything else is illusion.
P
So the rule is the lifeboat… and breaking it is stepping into the ocean?
M
Exactly.
Follow the rules and you float.
Break them and you sink instantly.
P
I will honor –40.
I will honor +40.
Nothing more. Nothing less.
M
Good. When a warrior accepts discipline,
victory becomes a habit.
MORAL OF THE STORY

The rule is not a cage — it's your lifeboat.

  • Feelings are wind. They change quickly.
  • Rules are anchors. They save you.
  • Accept only –40 or +40. Nothing else.
  • The market has no mercy — Moksha does.
