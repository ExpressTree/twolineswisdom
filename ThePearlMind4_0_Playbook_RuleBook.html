Section 1
Core Trading Principles — Trend Holding Framework
➤
Intent

“ThePearlMind 4.0 is not built to predict every tick. It is built to help the trader stay with the trend as long as the signal allows.”

Principle 1 — Stay in the Trend as per Signal

  • Every signal has a dedicated meaning and a dedicated action.
  • The model is a clarity framework, not a tip system.
  • The trader’s job is not to guess; the trader’s job is to follow the correct symbol, color and action.

Principle 2 — Use the + Until Trend Continues / –90 Template

  • Profit side remains open until the model shows trend reversal.
  • –90, or approximately –10% of the option premium, is the maximum educational loss per decision.
  • No jackpot chasing. The purpose is structured trend participation.

Principle 3 — Active Market Hours, High Signal Clarity

  • There can be many trades per day, week or month.
  • The major focus is to stay active during market hours without fear.
  • Outcome is measured through correct trend-following behaviour, not emotional PnL reactions.

The shift from 2.0 to 4.0 is simple: do not just catch 40 points — learn to hold the active trend with controlled risk.

Section 3
Entry Principles — When & How You Begin
➤
Intent

“Entry is not about catching tops or bottoms. In 4.0, entry is about acting quickly when the signal becomes clear.”

Principle 4 — Enter Only on Clear Entry Conditions

  • Arrow appears.
  • Confirmation line stabilises.
  • Option premium should be close to ₹800 where possible.
  • Avoid midway or chasing entries after large moves.

Principle 5 — The One-Minute Action Window

  • 4.0 does not keep the trader waiting for 3 minutes like 2.0.
  • Use approximately one minute to confirm direction, strike and mental readiness.
  • The purpose is quicker entry so the trader can capture more points in the trending direction.

Principle 6 — Direction Coding

  • Green = Upward direction.
  • Red = Downward direction.
  • Gray = Sluggish / ChopLock / neutral for options buyers.
  • Light Green or Light Red = exit/reversal context, not fresh new entry by itself.

ATM Option Stability

ATM options around ₹700–₹800 premium are preferred for educational consistency. OTM is used to explain decay risk.

Expiry Selection

1st–19th: current month expiry. 20th–month-end: next month expiry.

In 4.0, speed is useful only after clarity. Fast action without signal discipline is still emotional trading.

Section 4
Exit Principles — Trend Reversal, Trailing and Controlled Loss
➤
Intent

“ThePearlMind 4.0 teaches the trader to hold winning trends while keeping the losing side controlled.”

Principle 7 — Profit Side Remains Open Until Trend Reversal

  • Unlike 2.0, profit is not automatically closed at +40.
  • If profit goes beyond 80 points, the position can be trailed with approximately –10% of the option premium.
  • The objective is to stay with the trend until the model shows an opposite-color reversal or exit condition.

Principle 8 — Exit Only When Trend Reversal Appears

  • Exit the position when the model shows a valid signal of the opposite color.
  • Light signal inside ChopLock can mean closing the active position only.
  • Dark reversal signal can mean closing the existing position and opening the opposite side.

Principle 9 — –90 Maximum Educational Loss

  • –90 is the maximum educational loss per decision.
  • No widening of stop-loss.
  • Oversized losses such as –140/–150 may happen due to random moves beyond rule boundaries, but they should not become normal behaviour.

A strong trader is not the one who avoids every loss. A strong trader is the one who prevents one loss from becoming account damage.

Section 5
Avoid Principles — What Not to Do in 4.0
➤
Intent

“Most traders lose not because the signal was absent, but because discipline was absent.”

Principle 10 — Follow Time and Session Boundaries

  • First-candle action can be taken depending on the color of the trend.
  • No trades after 15:28 IST.
  • New day, new trade. No carry forward.

Principle 11 — Avoid Weak-Capital Trading

  • Avoid trading with cut-to-cut capital.
  • Suggested peaceful capital: at least ₹1,00,000 to trade 1 lot.
  • Capital pressure creates emotional exits and revenge entries.

Principle 12 — Do Not Mix Extra Indicators Mid-Session

  • Do not mix ThePearlMind with extra indicators during live execution.
  • The model will show trend continuation; kindly follow the right color for the right action.
  • Gray signals mean sluggish trending / ChopLock and are not good for options buyers.

Principle 13 — No Revenge Trading

  • After a loss, the mind wants to recover.
  • This is when maximum damage can happen.
  • The market is Nirantar; many future trends will come.

A missed trade is not a disaster. A revenge trade can become one.

Section 6
Position Sizing, Scaling and Mid-Trade Discipline
➤
Intent

“Scaling is not proof of confidence. Scaling is proof of past discipline.”

Principle 14 — Start with 1 Lot Until Discipline Proves Itself

  • Start with 1 lot until a full discipline cycle is completed.
  • No sudden lot-size increase.
  • Scaling must be earned, not forced.

Principle 15 — Scaling Trigger

  • After earning around ₹1,00,000 through consistent trend-following behaviour, scaling may be considered.
  • The focus is not one lucky trade; the focus is repeatable behaviour.

Principle 16 — Trust the Rule After Correct Entry

  • If entered correctly, trust the rule.
  • Let TP or SL hit based on the defined execution logic.
  • No midway panic exit.

Lot size is not mood. Lot size reflects past behaviour.

Principle 20 — Performance-Linked Offers and Responsibility

  • Performance-linked offers are fairness mechanisms, not promises.
  • All trading decisions rest with the participant, not with the tool.
  • ThePearlMind does not change your life. You change your life when you follow rules.

The goal of 4.0 is not more excitement. The goal is better holding, better exits, and better behaviour inside uncertainty.
