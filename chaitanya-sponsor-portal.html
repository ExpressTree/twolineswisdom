Chaitanya Devadhe — known as Mauli — is one of Maharashtra's most compelling emerging voices, born in Alandi Devachi, the sacred pilgrimage town near Pune. His roots in bhajan, abhang, and classical Indian music run deep, and his journey from Alandi to national television is a story Maharashtra is already proud of.

He rose to national recognition as a Top 10 Contestant on Superstar Singer (Sony TV), a Rising Star Season 2 Finalist with 94% public voting (Colors TV), and most recently as a Finalist on Indian Idol Season 15, earning applause from judges including Shreya Ghoshal, Vishal Dadlani, and Aditya Prateek Singh. He also made his Marathi film debut in Luckee (2019), directed by Sanjay Jadhav.

His multi-genre repertoire — spanning classical, Marathi folk, devotional, Bollywood, Sufi, and fusion — makes him a rare performer who can move audiences across all age groups within a single set.
